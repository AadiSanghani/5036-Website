What Is FIRST ?

FIRST is an international program for youth that recognizes the importance of science and technology and aims to promote interest in STEM fields. It operates four programs for youth in kindergarten through grade 12; FIRST LEGO League Jr, FIRST LEGO League, FIRST Tech Challenge and FIRST Robotics Competition. These programs prepare the youth of all ages for what is ahead of them and opens life-changing opportunities by allowing them to work side by side with likeminded individuals, scientists and engineers.

The FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) gives students in grades 9 through 12 a chance to work on a team to build a robot in 6 weeks. This team then competes in district events, hoping to secure a place at the District Championship, where teams from all across the province compete for a place at the World Championships, held in Detroit, Michigan or Houston, Texas. Throughout this season, it is important that all teams value the moral of FIRST; "Gracious Professionalism".

"FIRST is more than just Robots." - Dean Kamen, Founder of FIRST

About Us!

We are FRC TEAM 5036 The Robo Devils from Sir Wilfrid Laurier Collegiate Institute in Scarborough, Ontario. As a team, we strive to make everyone feel included and valued. From being a scout or on drive team, our team recognizes everyone's individual skills and takes those to our advantage. Our robotics program fosters creativity and innovation and promotes STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics).

Team 5036 goes beyond building robots. It is an incredible opportunity for students to develop lifelong skills, make unforgettable memories and friendships from teammates from other teams! To us, the idea of having a successful team is not only winning competitions and having a high ranking, it is the idea of teamwork and communication. Our definition of a team is not just a group of people trying to achieve a goal; it is a family that encourages everyone to be their absolute best. Our team may be small, but together we work hard as a family.
